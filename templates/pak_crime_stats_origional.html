Data Analysis Summary

Dive into the comprehensive data of crime statistics from the vibrant and diverse Punjab Province of Pakistan, spanning over a robust period of 13 years from 2002 to 2015. This dataset is a rich tapestry of information, encapsulating a grand total of 5,967 records, meticulously detailing the crime scenario across 9 administrative divisions and their 36 distinct districts. CrimeType: Unravel the 13 different threads of crime types, from the menacing shadows of murder to the stealthy whispers of theft, each telling its own tale of societal challenges. CrimeCount: Count every heartbeat of the society, quantifying the pulse of criminal activity, and providing a numerical essence to the safety and security of the region. Population: Anchor the analysis in reality, juxtaposing the crime counts against the backdrop of the population, providing a perceptive lens to understand the scale and impact of crime. This dataset is not just numbers and text; it’s a narrative of Punjab’s journey through time, a reflection of its struggles and resilience, and a map guiding us through the complex labyrinth of crime and society. So, embark on this analytical odyssey, unravel the stories hidden in the data, and uncover the secrets of Punjab's societal fabric.

High Overview of Data And their Insights


This data covers a span of 13 years across 36 districts. A total of 1606540 crimes were reported, with an average of 123580 crimes per year.

CrimeCount


The average number of crimes reported per record is approximately 269. The maximum number of crimes reported in a single record is 8,998.

Population


The average population per record is approximately 2.61 million. The maximum population listed is approximately 9.74 million.

Crime Trends Analysis


The total number of reported crimes has generally increased from 2002 to 2010, with a peak in 2010 at 143,343 reported crimes. After 2010, there is a notable decrease in the total number of reported crimes, reaching 124,038 in 2015.

Crime Type Analysis


Ordinary Thefts are the most commonly reported crime (335,151 incidents), followed by Hurt (270,920) and Motor Vehicle Theft (183,767).
Rioting has the lowest total number of reported incidents (1,948).


Findings Across Regional Analysis




By Division


Lahore Division has the highest total number of reported crimes (425,455), followed by Gujranwala (222,215) and Faisalabad (211,085).
Sahiwal has the lowest total number of reported crimes (86,619).

By District


Lahore District has the highest total number of reported crimes (284,267), followed by Faisalabad (134,218) and Multan (78,333).
Khushab has the lowest total number of reported crimes (10,148).
